# Paris, France — Travel Knowledge Base

## Overview

Paris, the City of Light, is one of the world's most visited destinations. Famous for art, fashion, gastronomy, and iconic architecture, it rewards both first-time visitors and repeat travelers. Best visited in April–June and September–October. August is hot and many locals leave, reducing crowds but closing some neighbourhood restaurants.

**Currency:** Euro (€). 1 USD ≈ 0.92 EUR (2024).
**Language:** French. English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants.
**Timezone:** CET (UTC+1), CEST in summer (UTC+2)

## Must-See Attractions

### Central Paris (1st–4th Arrondissements)
- **Eiffel Tower** — €29.40 summit entry (book weeks ahead online). Free to view from Trocadéro or Champ de Mars.
- **Louvre Museum** — €22. Arrive at opening (9 AM) or book timed entry online. Closed Tuesdays.
- **Notre-Dame Cathedral** — Exterior viewable free; interior restoration ongoing through 2024.
- **Île de la Cité** — Walk the island, visit Sainte-Chapelle (€13, stunning stained glass).

### Montmartre (18th)
- **Sacré-Cœur Basilica** — Free entry. Funicular ¥2 or walk up 222 steps. Best city views from the steps at sunset.
- **Place du Tertre** — Artists' square. Touristy but atmospheric.

### Marais (3rd–4th)
- **Musée Picasso** — €14. One of the world's best Picasso collections.
- **Place des Vosges** — Paris's oldest planned square. Free, beautiful for a picnic.
- **Centre Pompidou** — €15. Modern and contemporary art; rooftop views.

### Left Bank (5th–6th)
- **Musée d'Orsay** — €16. Impressionist masterpieces (Monet, Renoir, Van Gogh). Closed Mondays.
- **Luxembourg Gardens** — Free. Beautiful 17th-century formal garden.
- **Latin Quarter** — Walk Rue Mouffetard for street food and neighbourhood atmosphere.

## Local Food & Restaurants

### Budget (under €10)
- **Boulangeries** — Fresh baguettes (€1.20), croissants (€1.50), and jambon-beurre sandwiches (~€4). Best morning ritual in Paris.
- **L'As du Fallafel (Le Marais)** — Famous falafel wrap ~€7. Queue expected at lunch.
- **Café de la Mosquée** — Traditional mint tea and pastries near the Jardin des Plantes. ~€5.

### Mid-range (€10–€35)
- **Bistrot Paul Bert (11th)** — Classic Parisian bistro, steak frites ~€22. Book ahead.
- **Septime (11th)** — Natural wine bar and excellent lunch prix-fixe ~€28. Michelin-recommended.
- **Café de Flore / Les Deux Magots** — Historic literary cafés. Coffee €5, croque-monsieur €16. Worth one visit for the atmosphere.

### Splurge (€80+)
- **Le Jules Verne (Eiffel Tower)** — Michelin-starred, stunning views. Lunch ~€105.
- **Frenchie (2nd)** — Modern French tasting menu. ~€85/person. Book weeks ahead.

## Transport

### Airport to City
- **CDG Airport:** RER B train to Châtelet/Gare du Nord ~€11.80, 35–45 mins. Taxi €55–€65 flat rate to Left Bank/Right Bank (official rates, ask at taxi rank).
- **Orly Airport:** OrlyVal + RER B combination ~€12.10. Bus line 183 to Porte de Choisy, ~€2.10.

### City Transit
- **Carnet / Navigo Easy:** Buy a Navigo Easy card (€2) and load t+ tickets (€1.73 each or 10-pack €16.90) for metro/bus.
- **Navigo Week Pass:** €30/week — unlimited metro, RER, bus in zones 1–5. Best value for stays of 4+ days. Available Monday–Sunday only (buy on Mondays).
- **Metro:** 16 lines, runs 5:30 AM–1:15 AM (2:15 AM weekends). Fastest way to get around.
- **Vélib' Bikes:** City bike-share. Day pass €5, first 45-min ride free. Great for along the Seine.
- **Walking:** Central Paris is very walkable. Many sights 10–15 min apart on foot.

## Practical Tips

- **Museum Pass:** Paris Museum Pass (2 days €55, 4 days €70) covers 50+ museums with no queues. Worth it if you plan 3+ major museums.
- **Water:** Tap water is safe and excellent. Carry a reusable bottle and refill at Wallace fountains (green cast-iron fountains throughout the city).
- **Tipping:** Not obligatory. Round up bills or leave €1–€2 at cafés and restaurants. Never expected at bakeries.
- **Pickpockets:** Be alert around Eiffel Tower, Sacré-Cœur, and on metro line 1. Use a zipped bag, keep phone in pocket.
- **Sunday closures:** Many smaller shops and some restaurants close Sunday. Plan accordingly.
- **Bidet etiquette:** Don't step into the metro without validating your ticket — inspectors do check and fines are €50.
- **Language:** Attempt "Bonjour" and "Merci" — Parisians appreciate the effort and will switch to English.
- **Best day trips:** Versailles (40 min by RER C, €20 palace entry), Mont Saint-Michel (4h by TGV + shuttle), Loire Valley (1h to Tours by TGV, wine and châteaux).
