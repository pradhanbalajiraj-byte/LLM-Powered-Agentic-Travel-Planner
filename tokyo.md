# Tokyo, Japan — Travel Knowledge Base

## Overview

Tokyo is Japan's sprawling capital and one of the most dynamic cities on Earth. It blends ultra-modern skyscrapers with centuries-old temples, world-class cuisine, and an extraordinary transit system. Best visited in spring (March–May) for cherry blossoms or autumn (September–November) for cool weather and foliage.

**Currency:** Japanese Yen (JPY). 1 USD ≈ 150 JPY (2024).
**Language:** Japanese. English is widely understood in tourist areas and major stations.
**Timezone:** JST (UTC+9)

## Must-See Attractions

### Shinjuku
- **Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden** — ¥500 entry. Stunning garden with French, English, and Japanese sections. Best for cherry blossoms in April.
- **Kabukicho** — Tokyo's entertainment district. Free to walk; great for ramen and izakaya.
- **Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building** — Free observation deck with panoramic city views.

### Shibuya
- **Shibuya Crossing** — The world's busiest pedestrian scramble. Best experienced at night from the Starbucks or MAGNET by Shibuya 109 rooftop.
- **Meiji Jingu Shrine** — Free entry. A forested Shinto shrine in the heart of the city. Arrive early to see the quiet atmosphere.

### Asakusa
- **Senso-ji Temple** — Tokyo's oldest temple. Free entry; open 24 hours. Arrive before 8 AM to avoid crowds.
- **Nakamise Shopping Street** — Traditional snacks and souvenirs leading to Senso-ji.

### Harajuku
- **Takeshita Street** — Colourful youth fashion street. Free to walk.
- **Omotesando** — Tokyo's Champs-Élysées. Luxury boutiques and beautiful tree-lined boulevard.

### Akihabara
- **Electric Town** — Anime, manga, electronics, and gaming culture. Great for unique shopping.

### Odaiba
- **teamLab Planets** — Immersive digital art museum. ¥3,200. Book ahead online.
- **Odaiba Seaside Park** — Free. Rainbow Bridge views; great at sunset.

## Local Food & Restaurants

### Budget (under ¥1,000 / ~$7)
- **Ichiran Ramen** — Solo ramen cubicle experience. ~¥980 per bowl. Locations in Shinjuku, Shibuya, Harajuku.
- **Yoshinoya / Sukiya** — Gyudon (beef rice bowl) chains. ~¥400–¥600.
- **Convenience stores** — 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, and Lawson offer hot meals, onigiri, and excellent sandwiches for under ¥500.

### Mid-range (¥1,000–¥3,000 / $7–$20)
- **Katsukura (Shinjuku)** — Premium tonkatsu set meals. ~¥1,800.
- **Gonpachi Nishi-Azabu** — Izakaya chain. ~¥2,500–¥3,000 per person. Great atmosphere.
- **Tsukiji Outer Market** — Fresh sushi for breakfast. Set meals ¥1,500–¥2,500.

### Splurge (over ¥5,000 / $33+)
- **Sushi Saito** — Reservation required months in advance. One of Tokyo's best omakase.
- **Narisawa** — World-class innovative Japanese cuisine. ~¥30,000/person.

## Transport

### Airport to City
- **Narita Airport:** Narita Express (N'EX) to Shinjuku ¥3,070, ~80 mins. Limousine Bus ¥3,200, ~90–120 mins.
- **Haneda Airport:** Tokyo Monorail or Keikyu Line ¥600–¥700, ~35 mins to central Tokyo.

### City Transit
- **IC Cards (Suica / Pasmo):** Buy at any major station. Tap in/out on all trains, buses, and even convenience stores. Saves time and money. Load with ¥3,000–¥5,000.
- **JR Yamanote Line:** Loops around central Tokyo stopping at all major areas. Covered by IC card.
- **Tokyo Metro:** Day pass ¥800 (unlimited metro rides). Worthwhile if sightseeing across multiple wards.
- **Taxis:** Metered, reliable but expensive. ¥730 base fare + ¥90 per 250m. Use for late nights.
- **Apps:** Google Maps works excellently for route planning. Suica app on iPhone for digital IC card.

## Practical Tips

- **Cash is king:** Many restaurants and small shops are cash-only. Withdraw from 7-Eleven or Japan Post ATMs (they reliably accept foreign cards).
- **WiFi:** Rent a pocket WiFi at the airport (¥300–¥500/day) or buy a tourist SIM (Docomo/IIJmio, ¥2,000–¥4,000 for 15 days data).
- **Etiquette:** No eating while walking, no phone calls on trains, queue quietly. Tipping is not practiced and can be considered rude.
- **Trash cans:** Very rare on streets. Carry a small bag for rubbish.
- **Convenience stores:** Open 24/7. ATMs, food, medicine, printing, and bill payment all available.
- **Best day trips:** Nikko (2.5h), Kamakura (1h), Hakone (1.5h, Mt. Fuji views), Kyoto (2.5h by Shinkansen).
- **Useful apps:** Google Translate (camera mode for menus), Google Maps, Hyperdia (train schedules), TabiEats (restaurant finder).
